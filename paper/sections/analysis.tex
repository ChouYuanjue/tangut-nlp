\subsection{Reference-aligned diagnostics}

Table~\ref{tab:diagnostics} summarizes the output-side diagnostics. Here ``contamination'' flags outputs containing ASCII spillover, angle brackets, \texttt{[UNK]}, or known prompt-artifact tokens, and ``suffix preservation'' is computed on references containing at least one canonical title-suffix character; a prediction is counted as correct when it preserves all such reference-side suffix characters. The table caption gives the exact suffix list.

The frontier DeepSeek baseline is already a clean single model: 12/50 exact matches, 0 contamination, 24/27 suffix preservation, and a near-reference length ratio of 0.95. Within the local family, multitask SFT is the cleanest pure SFT system, and strict gap-0.4 DPO regularizes the outputs further. The 3-way open selector stays clean as well (12/50 exact, 0 contamination, 23/27 suffix preservation, 0.98 length ratio), while the 3-way catalog hybrid is cleaner still with 14/50 exact matches, 26/27 suffix preservation, 0 contamination, and the same 0.95 length ratio as the frontier baseline. The workflow gain therefore does not come from verbose rewriting; it comes from more accurate conservative title reconstruction.

\subsection{From complementarity to a deployable workflow}

The oracle analysis mattered because it showed that frontier and local candidates were not near-duplicates. Table~\ref{tab:oracle-complementarity} replaces the earlier progression view with a more useful workflow comparison.

\input{tables/oracle_complementarity}

The 3-way deterministic selector over frontier/UNK-SFT/MT-DPO already changes 12/50 cases without any closed model or text synthesis: it selects the frontier 38 times, UNK-SFT 8 times, and MT-DPO 4 times. The 3-way catalog hybrid goes further. It selects the frontier 36 times, MT-DPO 6 times, UNK-SFT 5 times, keeps one tie, and performs only 2 minimal repairs. Thus 48/50 outputs come from direct candidate selection rather than free rewriting, and local models contribute directly on 11 cases plus two repairs. The exact-match union of the three candidate sources is also 14/50, so the hybrid closes the exact oracle gap completely even though the oracle still retains headroom on chrF++ and overall score.

Pool expansion is informative in the opposite direction. Adding MT-SFT as a fourth candidate slightly raises raw chrF++ under GPT adjudication (35.64) but lowers reference-aware overall (2.98), exact matches (13/50), and suffix preservation (25/27); the adjudicator then falls back to the frontier 41 times and uses MT-SFT only once. The 4-way open selector behaves similarly, dropping from 2.90 to 2.70 overall. Candidate diversity therefore saturates quickly: the original 3-way pool is not arbitrary, but the best conservative operating point in this snapshot. The qualitative cases make the same point. The workflow restores \zh{五部經} instead of the frontier normalization \zh{五類經}, fixes the frontier truncation \zh{番} to \zh{番言金剛王乘根}, and replaces the frontier paraphrase \zh{德宜點義句顯宣論} with the more title-like \zh{正理滴之句義顯了}.

\subsection{Reference-aware judging changes the ranking}

The legacy LLM judge in the repository is reference-free, so we reran evaluation with a stricter judge that sees the Tangut input, the gold reference, and the candidate simultaneously.

\input{tables/reference_aware_eval}

Table~\ref{tab:reference-aware-eval} changes the ranking. Among single models, the frontier baseline remains the clear winner at 2.80 overall. Within the local learned family, the best overall score is shared by three multitask-base DPO variants at 2.22. Among workflows, the open selector already reaches 2.90 overall and the 3-way catalog hybrid 3.02, while the 4-way hybrid slips back to 2.98. The gains are concentrated where catalog conservatism matters most: on non-suffix titles the frontier baseline scores 2.39 overall and the 3-way hybrid 2.74, and on references of length $\le 6$ the frontier scores 2.94 and the hybrid 3.24. A local Qwen2.5-7B judge preserves the same frontier/open/hybrid/human ordering (4.20, 4.20, 4.24, 4.90), reducing concern that the workflow ranking is purely a GPT-family artifact.

\subsection{Metric alignment, robustness, and filtered DPO}

The \texttt{human\_reference} run is an important anchor. The gold reference receives perfect chrF++ and perfect reference-aware judge scores, but only moderate lexical coverage and catastrophic perplexity. Lexical coverage therefore measures overlap with dictionary-derived semantics rather than agreement with the gold title, while perplexity penalizes compact title style and historical transliterations. These metrics remain useful diagnostics, but they should not drive model selection in this task.

\input{tables/statistical_robustness}

The bootstrap results in Table~\ref{tab:statistical-robustness} clarify which claims are stable. The local chrF++ gain from multitask SFT to strict gap-0.4 sigmoid DPO remains above zero under resampling, and the catalog hybrid's chrF++ gain over the frontier baseline does as well. The judge-overall and exact-match gains are directionally positive but boundary-level on this 50-example test set. The overlap audit addresses a separate concern: there is zero exact source, target, or full-pair overlap across train/dev/test; zero exact overlap between test references and the 50k synthetic multitask targets; and zero exact overlap between the frontier few-shot exemplars and the test references. This does not prove complete immunity from leakage, but it rules out the simplest exact-copy explanation.

\input{tables/dpo_audit}

The DPO audit explains why strict filtering helps. Across all 3,867 preference pairs, the reward-chosen candidate is closer to a simple gold proxy only 71.3\% of the time, but this rises to 81.5\% for gaps $\ge 0.40$. The high-gap filter is therefore isolating cleaner labels rather than acting as a cosmetic hyperparameter. The reward-weight ablation sharpens the same conclusion: varying the coefficient on $\log \mathrm{ppl}$ from 0 to 0.02 causes zero pair-order flips relative to the current reward and changes the gap-0.4 pool only from 498 to 504 pairs. In other words, the small perplexity term is not selecting different high-gap pairs; the real stabilizer is still aggressive gap filtering on top of the multitask base. Once DPO is moved onto that base and filtered at 0.4, it stops behaving like a blanket negative result and becomes the strongest local model. The hybrid workflow does not erase this point; it depends on the local candidate pool being strong enough to contribute conservative alternatives in the first place.
