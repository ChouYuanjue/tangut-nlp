\subsection{Why the workflow gain is conservative rather than verbose}

Table~\ref{tab:diagnostics} summarizes the output-side diagnostics. Here ``contamination'' flags outputs containing ASCII spillover, angle brackets, \texttt{[UNK]}, or known prompt-artifact tokens, and ``suffix preservation'' is computed on references containing at least one canonical title-suffix character; a prediction is counted as correct when it preserves all such reference-side suffix characters. The table caption gives the exact suffix list.

The frontier baseline is already a clean single model: 12/50 exact matches, 0 contamination, 24/27 suffix preservation, and a near-reference length ratio of 0.95. Within the local family, multitask SFT is the cleanest pure SFT system, and strict gap-0.4 DPO regularizes the outputs further. The 3-way open selector stays clean as well, while the 3-way catalog hybrid is cleaner still with 14/50 exact matches, 26/27 suffix preservation, 0 contamination, and the same 0.95 length ratio as the frontier baseline. The gain therefore does not come from verbose rewriting; it comes from better conservative title reconstruction.

\subsection{Why three candidates are enough}

The oracle analysis mattered because it showed that frontier and local candidates were not near-duplicates. In the paper's interpretation, open reranking over that pool is the main reproducible workflow result, while the closed catalog adjudicator acts only as a probe of how much additional complementarity is present in the candidate pool.

The 3-way deterministic selector over frontier/UNK-SFT/MT-DPO already changes 12/50 cases without any closed model or text synthesis: it selects the frontier 38 times, UNK-SFT 8 times, and MT-DPO 4 times. The 3-way catalog adjudicator goes further. It selects the frontier 36 times, MT-DPO 6 times, UNK-SFT 5 times, keeps one tie, and performs only 2 minimal repairs. Thus 48/50 outputs come from direct candidate selection rather than free rewriting, and local models contribute directly on 11 cases plus two repairs. The exact-match union of the three candidate sources is also 14/50, so the closed adjudicator closes the exact oracle gap completely even though the oracle still retains headroom on chrF++ and overall score.

The new open reranking controls clarify what kind of complementarity is present. Plurality vote changes only 2/50 frontier outputs yet still reaches 13 exact matches and 2.94 overall, which means some local repairs are high-confidence enough to surface through exact agreement alone. A local Qwen reranker makes 6 switches, preserves 25/27 title suffixes, and stays contamination-free, but only reaches 2.82 overall; an open instruction model is therefore not yet a drop-in replacement for a task-aware selector. Pairwise chrF-MBR makes the opposite mistake. It switches 35/50 cases and climbs to 34.76 chrF++, but contamination rises to 6\%, length ratio to 1.17, and overall falls to 2.64. That contrast matters because it shows why generic consensus maximization is not enough for this task: it rewards agreement even when agreement comes from longer, less conservative titles.

The new pool-ablation control clarifies why this is a real complementarity story rather than a single lucky override. When we pair the frontier model with only one local candidate at a time, frontier+UNK, frontier+MT-SFT, and frontier+DPO all land at the same intermediate operating point: 30.05 chrF++, 13/50 exact matches, and zero contamination. The full 3-way selector then pushes chrF++ further to 32.62, even though exact match returns to 12/50. This pattern matters because it shows that the open selector is not just finding one hidden exact-match repair; it is combining multiple conservative local alternatives to improve aggregate title reconstruction more broadly.

Additional pool-expansion variants did not improve the conservative workflow and are therefore left out of the main narrative. The qualitative cases make the core point. The selector restores \zh{五部經} instead of the frontier normalization \zh{五類經}, fixes the frontier truncation \zh{番} to \zh{番言金剛王乘根}, and replaces the frontier paraphrase \zh{德宜點義句顯宣論} with the more title-like \zh{正理滴之句義顯了}. In other words, the gain is not that the system writes more, but that it sometimes knows when to stay closer to historical title structure.

\subsection{Why reference-aware evaluation is necessary}

The legacy LLM judge in the repository is reference-free, so we reran evaluation with a stricter judge that sees the Tangut input, the gold reference, and the candidate simultaneously.

\input{tables/reference_aware_eval}

Table~\ref{tab:reference-aware-eval} changes the ranking in the direction the task actually demands. Among single models, the frontier baseline remains the clear winner at 2.80 overall. Within the local learned family, MT-DPO g0.4 sig is strongest overall at 2.22. Among workflows, the open selector already reaches 2.90 overall and the closed 3-way catalog adjudicator 3.02. The gains are concentrated where catalog conservatism matters most: on non-suffix titles the frontier baseline scores 2.39 overall and the 3-way adjudicator 2.74, and on references of length $\le 6$ the frontier scores 2.94 and the adjudicator 3.24. A local Qwen2.5-7B judge preserves the same frontier/open/hybrid/human ordering (4.20, 4.20, 4.24, 4.90), reducing concern that the ranking is purely a GPT-family artifact.

The \texttt{human\_reference} run is an important anchor. The gold reference receives perfect chrF++ and perfect reference-aware judge scores, but only moderate lexical coverage and catastrophic perplexity. Lexical coverage therefore measures overlap with dictionary-derived semantics rather than agreement with the gold title, while perplexity penalizes compact title style and historical transliterations. These metrics remain useful diagnostics, but they should not drive model selection in this task.

\subsection{Why filtered DPO changes the local story}

Table~\ref{tab:statistical-robustness} summarizes the robustness checks around the local and workflow claims.

\input{tables/statistical_robustness}

The bootstrap results now mirror the open-control story rather than merely repeating the headline systems. The local chrF++ gain from multitask SFT to strict gap-0.4 sigmoid DPO remains above zero under resampling. On the workflow side, plurality vote and the guarded selector stay directionally positive but boundary-level, which is consistent with the small test set and the paper's deliberately conservative claim. The local Qwen reranker shows a small chrF++ interval entirely above zero, but its judge-overall gain does not; it is therefore a real open learned control, not yet a stronger task-level selector. Pairwise chrF-MBR is the clearest negative control: despite a large point estimate on chrF++, its interval spans zero and its judge-overall point estimate is negative. Only the closed adjudicator remains clearly positive on both chrF++ and judge-overall, which is why we report it as headroom rather than as the paper's main deployable result. The overlap audit addresses a separate concern: there is zero exact source, target, or full-pair overlap across train/dev/test, zero exact overlap between test references and the 50k synthetic multitask targets, and zero exact overlap between the frontier few-shot exemplars and the test references. This does not prove complete immunity from leakage, but it rules out the simplest exact-copy explanation.

\input{tables/dpo_audit}

The DPO audit then explains why the local story changes once the labels are cleaned. Across all 3,867 preference pairs, the reward-chosen candidate is closer to a simple gold proxy only 71.3\% of the time, but this rises to 81.5\% for gaps $\ge 0.40$. The high-gap filter is therefore isolating cleaner labels rather than acting as a cosmetic hyperparameter. The reward-weight ablation sharpens the same conclusion: varying the coefficient on $\log \mathrm{ppl}$ from 0 to 0.02 causes zero pair-order flips relative to the current reward and changes the gap-0.4 pool only from 498 to 504 pairs. In other words, the small perplexity term is not selecting different high-gap pairs; the real stabilizer is still aggressive gap filtering on top of the multitask base. Once DPO is moved onto that base and filtered at 0.4, it stops behaving like a blanket negative result and becomes the strongest local model. The hybrid workflow does not erase this point; it depends on the local candidate pool being strong enough to contribute conservative alternatives in the first place.

The selector sensitivity study provides a parallel robustness check on the workflow side. Across guarded 3-way configurations with frontier prior varied from 0 to 1.5, title-suffix bonus from 1.5 to 3.5, and switch margin from 2 to 4, the open selector stays within 32.33--33.48 chrF++, with 11--12 exact matches, 23--24 suffix preservations, and zero contamination. A more conservative margin-4 variant slightly increases chrF++ to 33.48 and restores suffix preservation to 24/27, but its reference-aware overall score drops from 2.90 to 2.86. We therefore keep the default guarded selector as the paper's designed overlap-oriented operating point: plurality vote is a strong minimalist control, local Qwen reranking is an open learned control, and pairwise MBR is a useful negative control, but the guarded selector best balances large overlap gains against conservative title quality.
