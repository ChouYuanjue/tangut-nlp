\section{Analysis}

\subsection{Why the workflow gain is conservative rather than verbose}

Table~\ref{tab:diagnostics} summarizes the output-side diagnostics. Here ``contamination'' is a deterministic binary flag rather than a tuned threshold: an output is contaminated iff it contains any Latin alphabetic character, angle-bracket markup, an explicit \texttt{[UNK]} token, or one of the small prompt-artifact lexemes observed in the legacy runs. ``Suffix preservation'' is computed on references containing at least one canonical title-suffix character; a prediction is counted as correct when it preserves all such reference-side suffix characters. The table caption gives the exact suffix list.

The frontier baseline is already a clean single model: 12/50 exact matches, 0 contamination, 24/27 suffix preservation, and a near-reference length ratio of 0.95. Within the local family, multitask SFT is the cleanest pure SFT system, and strict gap-0.4 DPO regularizes the outputs further. The 3-way open selector stays clean as well, while the 3-way catalog hybrid is cleaner still with 14/50 exact matches, 26/27 suffix preservation, 0 contamination, and the same 0.95 length ratio as the frontier baseline. The gain therefore does not come from verbose rewriting; it comes from better conservative title reconstruction.

Table~\ref{tab:failure-taxonomy} then operationalizes the companion error-taxonomy question. We group non-exact outputs into contamination, truncation, over-expansion, title-form slips (suffix drop or numeral mismatch), and residual content drift. This matters because the failure profiles are not interchangeable. Legacy DPO is the only representative system with material artifact leakage (9/50), while frontier prompting is already clean and mostly fails through content drift plus a single severe truncation. MT-SFT and MT-DPO remain title-like more often than the legacy DPO checkpoint, but they still expose more suffix and numeral slips than the frontier baseline. The guarded open selector inherits the frontier cleanliness, removes the frontier truncation case, and keeps the remaining errors in the same conservative title-reconstruction regime; the closed catalog adjudicator reduces the length-error bucket further, but we still report it only as headroom because its decision rule is not fully open.

\subsection{Why three candidates are enough}

The oracle analysis mattered because it showed that frontier and local candidates were not near-duplicates. The 3-way deterministic selector over frontier/UNK-SFT/MT-DPO already changes 12/50 cases without any closed model or text synthesis, while the closed catalog adjudicator still selects directly from the pool on 48/50 items and performs only 2 minimal repairs. The exact-match union of the three candidate sources is also 14/50, so the closed adjudicator closes the exact oracle gap without relying on free rewriting.

The open reranking controls then clarify what kind of complementarity is present. Plurality vote changes only 2/50 frontier outputs yet still reaches 13 exact matches and 2.94 overall, so some local repairs are strong enough to surface through exact agreement alone. A local Qwen reranker remains clean but only reaches 2.82 overall, while pairwise chrF-MBR over-switches, raising contamination and lowering judged quality despite higher overlap. The full 3-way selector matters because it combines multiple conservative local alternatives rather than relying on one lucky exact-match override. Qualitatively, it restores \zh{五部經} instead of the frontier normalization \zh{五類經} and fixes the frontier truncation \zh{番} to \zh{番言金剛王乘根}.

\subsection{Why reference-aware evaluation is necessary}

The legacy LLM judge in the repository is reference-free, so we reran evaluation with a stricter judge that sees the Tangut input, the gold reference, and the candidate simultaneously.

\input{tables/reference_aware_eval}

Table~\ref{tab:reference-aware-eval} changes the ranking in the direction the task actually demands. Among single models, the frontier baseline remains the clear winner at 2.80 overall; within the local learned family, MT-DPO g0.4 sig is strongest at 2.22. Among workflows, the open selector reaches 2.90 overall and the closed 3-way catalog adjudicator 3.02, with especially large gains on non-suffix and very short titles. A local Qwen2.5-7B judge preserves the same frontier/open/hybrid/human ordering (4.20, 4.20, 4.24, 4.90), reducing concern that the ranking is purely a GPT-family artifact.

The \texttt{human\_reference} run is an important anchor. The gold reference receives perfect chrF++ and perfect reference-aware judge scores, but only moderate lexical coverage and catastrophic perplexity. Lexical coverage therefore measures overlap with dictionary-derived semantics rather than agreement with the gold title, while perplexity penalizes compact title style and historical transliterations. These metrics remain useful diagnostics, but they should not drive model selection in this task.

Table~\ref{tab:uncertainty-intervals} makes the small-test-set issue explicit rather than implicit. On Tangut, the exact-match and chrF++ intervals are wide enough that the paper should avoid triumphalist claims about small workflow deltas. This is why the Tangut result is now phrased conservatively: open reranking is directionally helpful and reproducible, but only the closed adjudicator is clearly separated from the frontier baseline on paired chrF++ bootstrap. The paired bootstrap comparisons mirror the same story: filtered DPO improves the local base, plurality vote and the guarded selector remain directionally positive but boundary-level, and pairwise chrF-MBR is a useful negative control because its overlap gain does not survive judged-quality checks. The overlap audit addresses a separate concern: there is zero exact source, target, or full-pair overlap across train/dev/test, zero exact overlap between test references and the 50k synthetic multitask targets, and zero exact overlap between the frontier few-shot exemplars and the test references.

The DPO audit then explains why the local story changes once the labels are cleaned. Across all 3,867 preference pairs, the reward-chosen candidate is closer to a simple gold proxy only 71.3\% of the time, but this rises to 81.5\% for gaps $\ge 0.40$, so the high-gap filter is isolating cleaner labels rather than acting as a cosmetic hyperparameter. The reward-design ablation sharpens the same conclusion: removing the perplexity tie-breaker changes essentially nothing, whereas adding multitask \texttt{<literal>} overlap yields much cleaner but much sparser labels. We therefore interpret the DPO result narrowly: strict filtering rescues a noisy lexical reward well enough to make DPO competitive, but a stronger structure-aware reward remains future work. Guarded-selector sensitivity runs show the same kind of stability on the workflow side, so we keep the default selector as the overlap-oriented open operating point.
