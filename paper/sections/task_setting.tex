\section{Task Setting}

The paper studies Tangut short-text translation under two coupled constraints. First, supervision is tiny: the repository contains only 491 real pairs, split into 400 training examples, 41 development examples, and 50 test examples. Second, the outputs are short and often title-like. Success therefore depends on precise lexical choice and compact formatting rather than generic sentence fluency.

This makes the task narrow but meaningful. We ask how to recover short historical titles under only 400 real training examples, not how to solve open-domain Tangut translation. To train local models at all, we use synthetic data as structured proxies: each SFT branch adds 50,000 synthetic Tangut-Chinese pairs built from monolingual historical Chinese and dictionary knowledge, then combines them with the real training set upsampled by a factor of 10. These synthetic examples are not claims about faithful Tangut reconstruction; they are judged only by held-out performance on real Tangut titles.

We also audit data hygiene conservatively. There is zero exact source, target, or full-pair overlap across train/dev/test, zero exact overlap between the test references and the final targets of the 50k synthetic multitask corpus, and zero exact overlap between the frontier few-shot exemplars and the test references. Approximate $n$-gram overlap between synthetic multitask targets and the test references is also low, so the synthetic set is not acting as a near-duplicate cache.

Evaluation is the second bottleneck. We therefore treat chrF++, exact match, contamination, suffix preservation, and the reference-aware judge as primary evidence, while lexical coverage, perplexity, and the legacy reference-free judge remain diagnostic. chrF++ is a reasonable overlap anchor for this setting because character-level scoring is relatively stable under orthographic variation \cite{popovic2017chrfpp}, whereas perplexity and lexical coverage can mis-rank even the gold reference in this title-heavy regime \cite{rei2020comet, kepler2019openkiwi, zouhar2024cometpitfalls, pombal2025mint}.
