\section{Discussion and Limitations}

This paper is a question-driven case study of what changes once a strong frontier model is already available under extreme scarcity. With only 491 real pairs, the issue is not whether one local model can beat that frontier system outright, but what local signal remains worth learning. In this repository, the answer is structural conservatism: alignment-scaffolded local training produces title candidates that fail differently from frontier prompting and are useful mainly as complements. The most reproducible positive claim is therefore workflow-level, not single-model replacement.

The scope nevertheless remains narrow. The current data support Tangut short-text translation, especially title-like strings, not open-domain sentence translation. The strongest adjudicator and primary reference-aware judge are still closed models, and the new oracle-bone result is still a small canonicalized decipherment probe rather than a matched short-title benchmark. The next steps follow directly from these limits: distill the closed adjudication signal into stronger open reranking, expand the oracle side from canonicalized OBIMD$\rightarrow$EVOBC probes to richer matched oracle short-text references, and only then run targeted expert evaluation.
