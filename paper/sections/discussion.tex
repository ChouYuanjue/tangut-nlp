\section{Discussion and Limitations}

The practical reading of the paper is simple. If a user can make only one model call, the DeepSeek-V3.2 frontier baseline is the best single system we measured. If the target use case is scholar-facing short-title reconstruction, however, the stronger claim is the workflow claim: conservative local candidates help because they sometimes preserve short historical title chains that frontier prompting normalizes, truncates, or over-regularizes.

That distinction makes the contribution more general, not less. The local models matter not because they beat a frontier model head-to-head, but because they diversify the candidate pool with structurally conservative hypotheses. A similar recipe should transfer beyond Tangut book titles: train local models to stay close to title structure, then adjudicate with a title-aware reranker rather than a generic fluency prior.

The scope should also be explicit. The current dataset supports Tangut short-text translation, especially title-like strings, not general open-domain Tangut sentence translation. The synthetic corpus is a structural training proxy rather than realistic pseudo-Tangut, and the strongest gains are concentrated on short and mid-length items. We therefore treat multilingual-transfer and frontier-only sampled reranking baselines as important next controls rather than settled negatives.

Two limitations matter most. First, the real dataset is extremely small, so judge and exact-match gains remain borderline even when chrF++ gains are robust. Second, the primary adjudicator and judge are closed models, and we do not yet provide blinded expert validation. The deterministic open selector narrows this reproducibility gap but does not close it; the repository should therefore release the frontier prompt script, exemplar IDs, adjudication JSON, and per-item predictions alongside the paper. The most useful next steps are a sampled frontier-only MBR/QE reranking control, distilling the adjudicator into an open local reranker, validating the judge on a small expert-annotated subset, and testing transfer to other historical short-form datasets.
