\section{Discussion and Limitations}

This paper is a question-driven case study of what changes once a strong frontier model is already available under extreme scarcity. With only 491 real pairs, the scientific issue is not whether one local model can beat that frontier system outright, but what local signal remains worth learning. In this repository, the answer is structural conservatism: alignment-scaffolded local training produces title candidates that fail differently from frontier prompting and are useful mainly as complements. The most reproducible positive claim is therefore workflow-level. Several open rerankers already improve on the frontier baseline, but they do so with different biases: vote exposes high-confidence local repairs, the local Qwen reranker shows that a fully open learned selector is feasible, and chrF-MBR shows that generic consensus can be misaligned with conservative title reconstruction. The stronger closed adjudicator is reported only as evidence of remaining headroom.

The new EVOBC adapter plus the 200-item OBIMD-derived canonicalized probe narrows one portability concern in precisely the direction relevant to the main claim. This is not just an asset-conversion exercise: the test uses real OBIMD sequences, a weaker shared-label bridge, and no oracle-specific retuning of the main workflow. Even under that conservative setup, dictionary-grounded prompting remains the best single model, but a simple open 2-way hybrid still adds four exact matches over the prompt baseline and reduces contamination to 0.5\%. The transfer claim is therefore not that local models dominate across scripts; it is that complementary repair behavior survives a second non-Unicode symbol inventory.

The scope nevertheless remains narrow. The current data support Tangut short-text translation, especially title-like strings, not open-domain sentence translation. The strongest adjudicator and primary reference-aware judge are still closed models, and the new oracle-bone result is still a small canonicalized decipherment probe rather than a matched short-title translation benchmark. Expert review is essential for eventual scholar-facing use, but it answers a different question from the benchmark comparison resolved here. The next steps follow directly from these limits: distill the closed adjudication signal into stronger open reranking, expand the oracle side from canonicalized OBIMD$\rightarrow$EVOBC probes to richer matched oracle short-text references, and then run targeted expert evaluation as follow-up external validation.
