\section{Main Results}

Table~\ref{tab:main-results} separates three questions that earlier drafts blurred together: what the strongest prompt-only single model can do, what the strongest open/local trainable model can do, and what a real hybrid workflow can do. The first point is straightforward. The stronger frontier objection is real: the DeepSeek-V3.2 few-shot dictionary baseline remains the strongest single model.

The positive local-method result still lies inside the trainable regime. Structured multitask SFT is the best supervised base, and DPO helps only after it is moved onto that base and filtered much more aggressively. MT-DPO g0.4 sig is the strongest learned local model: raising the minimum reward gap from 0.2 to 0.4 shrinks the preference set from 1,996 to 498 pairs, lifts chrF++ from 20.93 to 30.01, and restores the length ratio from 1.74 to 1.12. The strict robust variant is slightly weaker on chrF++, and the legacy DPO checkpoint remains a genuine negative control.

The workflow result should be read separately from the single-model result. Table~\ref{tab:oracle-complementarity} now gives the consolidated workflow view. A fully open 3-way selector over frontier, UNK-SFT, and MT-DPO g0.4 sig already reaches 32.62 chrF++ and 2.90 reference-aware overall, so part of the gain is reproducible without closed adjudication. The 3-way catalog hybrid is the best conservative operating point at 34.87 chrF++, 3.02 overall, and 14/50 exact matches. Expanding the candidate pool with MT-SFT is informative but not better in the same sense: the 4-way catalog hybrid slightly raises chrF++ to 35.64, yet lowers overall to 2.98, exact matches to 13/50, and suffix preservation to 25/27. The 4-way open selector is also slightly worse than the 3-way open selector (32.47 vs.\ 32.62 chrF++; 2.70 vs.\ 2.90 overall). More candidates therefore raise overlap more easily than they improve conservative title reconstruction.

These disagreements are why the paper should not rely on a single automatic metric. chrF++, diagnostics, and the reference-aware judge reward different parts of the short-title problem. The defensible claim is therefore layered rather than absolutist: strict gap-filtered multitask DPO is the best local training recipe, and conservative 3-way adjudication turns that local complementarity into the strongest workflow.

The statistical analysis makes those layers more precise. Under 5,000 paired bootstrap resamples, the best local DPO model improves over multitask SFT by +4.02 chrF++ with a 95\% confidence interval of [0.84, 7.80]. More importantly for the workflow claim, the 3-way catalog hybrid improves over the frontier baseline by +6.33 chrF++ with a 95\% confidence interval of [2.10, 11.67]. The gains in reference-aware overall (+0.22, 95\% CI [0.00, 0.48]) and exact match (+0.04, 95\% CI [0.00, 0.10]) are positive but boundary-level under this 50-example test set. The open selector is directionally better than the frontier baseline but not statistically robust at this sample size (+4.08 chrF++, 95\% CI [$-2.28$, 10.64]). The right claim is therefore a robust overlap gain for the full hybrid, a reproducible directional gain for the open selector, and a conservative 3-way operating point rather than ``more candidates is always better.''
