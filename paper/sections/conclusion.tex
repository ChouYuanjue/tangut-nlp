\section{Conclusion}

The current repository already supports a coherent paper, but the final message is no longer ``SFT good, DPO bad.'' Structured multitask synthetic supervision remains the strongest pure SFT baseline. Pure-input training is helpful, but noisy semantic projection does not yet outperform the simpler \texttt{UNK} strategy. On top of that stronger multitask base, however, stricter gap-filtered DPO now gives the strongest learned systems: \texttt{final\_gap04\_multitask\_sigmoid} reaches the highest learned-system chrF++ (30.01) and ties for the best reference-aware overall score (2.22), while \texttt{final\_gap04\_multitask\_robustwpo} reaches the same overall score with the strongest title-style fitness. The legacy \texttt{final\_v2} checkpoint remains a genuine negative control, so the positive result is conditional on both the base model and the pair filter. Several reference-free metrics also remain misaligned enough to mis-rank the human reference. Together, these findings argue for a two-stage practical lesson: in ultra-low-resource Tangut short-text translation, explicit structural supervision is the right base recipe, aggressively filtered preference optimization can improve on it, and evaluation must stay anchored to reference-aware evidence.
