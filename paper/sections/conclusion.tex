\section{Conclusion}

Tangut short-text translation is difficult here not only because the dataset is tiny, but because the desired outputs are short historical titles that generic MT objectives and metrics do not model well. We therefore asked a narrower question: after strong dictionary-grounded frontier prompting is already in place, what remains useful about local training? The answer is that frontier prompting is the single-model upper bound, while structured local training remains valuable because it supplies conservative complementary titles that an open selector can exploit. The broader lesson is to frame ultra-low-resource historical translation as workflow design under reference-aware evaluation rather than as a head-to-head attempt to make one local model beat a frontier API.

The immediate next step is to turn the closed adjudication signal into stronger open reranking and to validate the workflow on a small expert-annotated subset.
